# Glossary

- `ADR`: Architecture Decision Record. A durable record of an architectural or governance decision.
- `agent`: A non-human software actor operating on repository artifacts, for example `agent:repo-bootstrapper`.
- `approval`: An explicit authorization by an authorized human identity to allow a task to advance.
- `audit`: An independent inspection of task records, controls, and evidence.
- `business owner`: The human accountable for business intent and risk acceptance.
- `change type`: A normalized category of change such as `documentation`, `code`, `configuration`, `security-control`, or `governance`.
- `control`: A required preventive, detective, or corrective measure tied to risk.
- `evidence`: Commands, outputs, screenshots, logs, test results, diffs, links, and rationales that substantiate what occurred.
- `exception`: A deviation from standard control requirements, typically documented through a waiver.
- `executor`: The human or agent performing the approved work.
- `final disposition`: The terminal outcome for a task such as `implemented`, `rejected`, `withdrawn`, or `superseded`.
- `human oversight`: A control requiring named human review or approval of task scope, execution, or outcome.
- `requester`: The human asking for work to be considered.
- `review`: A structured inspection of the completed work and its evidence against requirements.
- `risk tier`: Normalized impact level: `low`, `medium`, `high`, or `critical`.
- `segregation of duties`: Separation of incompatible responsibilities across distinct identities.
- `task`: A repository-scoped unit of governed AI-assisted work represented by a task folder.
- `waiver`: A time-bounded, approved exception to a required control.
